AI resume keyword optimization works best as a comparison exercise, not as a word-count game. Start with a clear base
resume, paste the target job description, and look for vocabulary that describes work you have actually done.

Useful keyword checks usually separate three groups: terms already supported by your resume, terms missing from the
resume but true to your experience, and terms that are important to the job but not part of your background. Only the
second group should change your resume.

When you add a keyword, attach it to evidence. A backend role might mention queueing, observability, PostgreSQL,
incident response, or API design. Those terms belong in bullets that explain systems you shipped, dashboards you used,
or reliability problems you solved.

Avoid dumping every matched phrase into a skills section. Recruiters and ATS systems both benefit from context. A
tailored Markdown resume should become more specific after keyword review, not louder.
