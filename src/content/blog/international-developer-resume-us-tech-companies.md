US tech resumes are usually direct, compact, and evidence-heavy. A one-page format is common for early and mid-career
candidates, while senior candidates can use two pages when the extra space adds clear value.

Use a simple header with name, email, location, LinkedIn, GitHub, and portfolio if relevant. Do not include personal
details that are not normally used in US applications, such as photo, marital status, date of birth, or full home address.

Translate titles and education into plain terms a recruiter can understand quickly. If a company, university, or project
is less familiar to a US audience, use the bullet content to explain scale: users, revenue, systems, team size, traffic,
latency, cost, or delivery impact.

The strongest international resumes make context easy. They do not assume the reader knows the local market, and they do
not hide the important results behind broad claims.
