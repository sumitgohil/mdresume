AI resume bullet rewrites are useful when a bullet has real substance but weak framing. They are risky when they invent
scope, inflate ownership, or make every line sound like the same polished sentence.

Before asking for rewrites, write the rough truth yourself: what you built, who used it, the stack or system involved,
and what changed. Then ask for suggestions that preserve the facts while improving clarity, action verbs, and outcome
language.

A good rewritten bullet usually has four parts: the action, the object, the technical or business context, and the
result. For example, "improved onboarding" becomes stronger when it names the flow, the React or API work involved,
and the measured reduction in support tickets or setup time.

Do not accept every suggestion. Keep bullets short enough to scan, remove inflated adjectives, and make sure the final
line still sounds like something you can defend in an interview.

In a Markdown resume, rewrites are easy to compare. Keep the original bullet nearby, evaluate two or three options, and
commit only the version that improves specificity without changing the facts.
