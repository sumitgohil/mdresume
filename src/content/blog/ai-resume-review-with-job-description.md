The best AI resume review starts with a real job description. Without one, the review can only give generic advice.
With one, it can compare your resume against the role's responsibilities, seniority signals, tools, and domain language.

Read the job description once yourself first. Mark the requirements that are genuinely important, then run the AI review
to check whether your resume already supports them. You are looking for missing evidence, not a perfect match score.

Strong review prompts should ask for specific gaps: missing technologies you have used, weak bullets that need clearer
outcomes, responsibilities that are hidden in the wrong section, and claims that need more proof. Ask the AI to separate
"missing but true" from "not present in your experience."

After the review, edit selectively. Add one or two bullets where the job description exposes an important gap. Reorder
skills only when it helps scanning. Leave out anything you cannot explain confidently.

diff, preview the result, and export only when the resume is both targeted and honest.
